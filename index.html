Otters

The world's greatest animal, otters. Why are they the greatest? Well, just have a look here.
They have a favorite rock

Otters use a small rock to crack open clams and crabs for food. Many of them have a favorite rock that they keep in their underarm pockets that they carry everywhere.

Learn More
They hold hands when they sleep

This is to avoid drifting away while they are sleeping or relaxing together. Otters have a great fear of losing their family members in general.

Learn More
Sea otters have the thickest fur of any animal

Their fur has between 600,000 to 1,000,000 hair follicles per square inch. They have water resistent fur to provide insulation.

Learn More
Otters' lung capacity is 2.5 times greather than of similar-sized mammals

Most otters can hold their breath for more than 5 minutes at a time while river otters can hold for up to 8 minutes.

Learn More
Otters are a keystone species

A keystone species are vital when it comes to keeping an ecosystem functioning. Sea otters help control the sea urchin population, which prevents urchins from overgrazing.

Learn More
Otters eat a lot

Sea otters can eat up to 20-25% of their body weight on one day. They would hunt for 3 to 5 hours in a day and when nursing a youngling, they would hunt for up to 8 hours!
